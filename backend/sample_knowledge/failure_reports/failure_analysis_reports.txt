FAILURE ANALYSIS REPORT: FA-2024-007
Equipment: Continuous Caster #1 (CCM-001), Strand 2
Component: Mould copper plate (narrow face)
Failure Date: 2024-06-22
Analysis Date: 2024-07-05

FAILURE DESCRIPTION:
Shell sticking led to breakout on strand 2 at approximately 1.5 meters below the mould exit. Liquid steel escaped through a tear in the solidified shell.

EXAMINATION FINDINGS:
1. Mould copper plate showed deep wear grooves (>2mm) at meniscus level
2. Surface coating (Cr+Ni) was completely worn through in the lower 200mm
3. Mould oscillation marks on removed shell piece showed irregular spacing — indicating oscillation irregularity
4. Oil pump for mould oscillation showed internal gear wear — flow rate reduced to 60% of specification

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
Primary: Mould oscillation pump degradation led to inconsistent lubrication at the mould-shell interface
Contributing: Copper plate wear had reduced mould thermal extraction efficiency
Contributing: Sticker detection response time (3 seconds) was too slow to prevent breakout

FAILURE MODE CLASSIFICATION:
- Lubrication failure (primary)
- Wear-out (contributing)
- Protection system inadequacy (contributing)

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Install redundant oscillation pump with automatic switchover — COMPLETED
2. Reduce sticker detection response time to 1 second — COMPLETED
3. Establish maximum copper plate wear limit and track remaining life — COMPLETED
4. Add oscillation pump flow rate to continuous monitoring — COMPLETED
5. Consider upgrading to hydraulic oscillation (eliminating mechanical pump) — UNDER EVALUATION

ESTIMATED COST OF FAILURE: $280,000 (lost production + repair + new parts)
RECURRENCE PROBABILITY (if actions not taken): HIGH (within 12 months)
RECURRENCE PROBABILITY (after actions): LOW

FAILURE ANALYSIS REPORT: FA-2024-012
Equipment: Blast Furnace #1 (BF-001)
Component: Tuyere #7 — Cooling water circuit
Failure Date: 2024-03-15
Analysis Date: 2024-03-25

FAILURE DESCRIPTION:
Tuyere #7 experienced complete burn-through. Cooling water immediately vaporized on contact with hot metal, creating steam explosion risk. Emergency plug installed within 10 minutes.

EXAMINATION FINDINGS:
1. Tuyere nose showed complete melting in lower quadrant
2. Cooling water channel was 70% blocked with calcium carbonate scale
3. Water flow to tuyere #7 had been declining over 6 weeks (from 120 L/min to 45 L/min)
4. Other tuyeres in same cooling circuit showed 20-40% scale buildup

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
Primary: Calcium carbonate scale accumulation in tuyere cooling channels due to hard water
Contributing: Water treatment chemical dosing pump had been running at reduced capacity
Contributing: No individual tuyere flow monitoring installed — only circuit total flow monitored

FAILURE MODE CLASSIFICATION:
- Fouling/contamination (primary)
- Maintenance deficiency (contributing)
- Monitoring gap (contributing)

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Install individual flow meters on each tuyere cooling line — COMPLETED
2. Upgrade water treatment to include online hardness monitoring — COMPLETED
3. Implement weekly water quality spot-checks at tuyere level — COMPLETED
4. Set alarm for individual tuyere flow drop >20% from baseline — COMPLETED
5. Schedule annual chemical cleaning of all tuyere cooling circuits — IN PROGRESS

ESTIMATED COST OF FAILURE: $85,000 (parts + downtime + emergency response)
RECURRENCE PROBABILITY (after actions): VERY LOW

FAILURE ANALYSIS REPORT: FA-2025-003
Equipment: Hot Rolling Mill (HRM-001)
Component: F3 Finishing Stand — Work roll bearing
Failure Date: 2025-03-20 (predicted), prevented via PdM
Analysis Date: 2025-03-25

FAILURE DESCRIPTION:
Predictive maintenance (vibration monitoring) detected early-stage outer race defect on F3 stand upper work roll bearing. Bearing was proactively replaced during planned roll change, preventing unplanned failure.

EXAMINATION FINDINGS:
1. Outer race showed fatigue spalling — typical pattern for bearing approaching end-of-life
2. Spalling area: approximately 8mm x 3mm on load zone
3. Inner race condition: satisfactory, minor polishing
4. Rolling elements: 2 out of 24 rollers showed surface distress
5. Lubricant condition: adequate, no contamination detected
6. Bearing had been in service for 14 months (expected life: 12-18 months)

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
Primary: Normal fatigue wear — bearing reached design life under operating loads
Contributing: None — this was a normal wear-out failure mode

FAILURE MODE CLASSIFICATION:
- Fatigue (normal end-of-life)

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Maintain vibration-based monitoring program — ONGOING
2. Set automated trending alarm at BPFO amplitude increase of 6dB — COMPLETED
3. Consider upgrading to hybrid ceramic bearings for extended life — UNDER EVALUATION
4. Document bearing life data for improved replacement forecasting — COMPLETED

KEY LEARNING: Predictive maintenance program successfully detected bearing defect 4-6 weeks before catastrophic failure would have occurred. Estimated savings: $350,000 in avoided unplanned downtime and consequential damage.

FAILURE ANALYSIS REPORT: FA-2025-008
Equipment: Hydraulic Press 2000T (HP-001)
Component: Main cylinder piston seal
Failure Date: 2025-02-18
Analysis Date: 2025-03-01

FAILURE DESCRIPTION:
Press unable to maintain forging position. Cylinder drift of 2mm/min under load. Oil temperature elevated to 62°C (normal: 40-50°C).

EXAMINATION FINDINGS:
1. Piston U-cup seal showed extensive extrusion and wear
2. Backup ring was fragmented — no longer providing anti-extrusion support
3. Cylinder bore showed minor scoring (within tolerable limits after honing)
4. Piston rod surface: satisfactory, chrome intact
5. Seal material: polyurethane, 28 months in service (expected life: 24-36 months)

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
Primary: Normal wear-out of polyurethane piston seal
Contributing: Backup ring material selection — PTFE backup ring became brittle over time, reducing anti-extrusion support and accelerating U-cup wear

FAILURE MODE CLASSIFICATION:
- Wear-out (primary)
- Material degradation (contributing)

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Reduce seal replacement interval from 36 months to 24 months — COMPLETED
2. Upgrade backup ring material from PTFE to glass-filled PTFE — COMPLETED
3. Implement cylinder drift monitoring with automated alarm — COMPLETED
4. Add oil temperature trending to predictive maintenance program — COMPLETED

ESTIMATED COST OF FAILURE: $45,000 (seal replacement + downtime + production loss)

FAILURE ANALYSIS REPORT: FA-2025-015
Equipment: Continuous Caster #1 (CCM-001), Strand 5
Component: Electromagnetic Stirrer (EMS) coil
Failure Date: 2025-08-30
Analysis Date: 2025-09-15

FAILURE DESCRIPTION:
EMS on strand 5 ceased functioning. Detected via product quality deterioration — macro etch samples showed increased center porosity and carbon segregation.

EXAMINATION FINDINGS:
1. EMS coil insulation resistance measured at 0.2 MΩ (specification: >100 MΩ)
2. Water ingress found inside coil housing
3. Cooling tube fitting at coil inlet had developed a hairline crack
4. Estimated water exposure duration: 2-3 weeks (based on insulation degradation rate)
5. Other strand EMS units tested — all satisfactory (>500 MΩ)

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
Primary: Cooling water leak through cracked tube fitting caused insulation breakdown
Contributing: Fitting material (brass) susceptible to stress corrosion cracking in industrial atmosphere
Contributing: No moisture detection system in EMS housing

FAILURE MODE CLASSIFICATION:
- Electrical insulation breakdown (primary)
- Corrosion-induced leak (contributing)
- Monitoring gap (contributing)

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Replace brass fittings with stainless steel on all EMS cooling connections — IN PROGRESS
2. Install moisture sensors in all EMS housings — COMPLETED
3. Add monthly insulation resistance measurement to PM schedule — COMPLETED
4. Maintain 1 spare EMS coil assembly in inventory — COMPLETED

ESTIMATED COST OF FAILURE: $120,000 (coil replacement + quality downgrade of affected billets)

SUMMARY OF KEY FAILURE PATTERNS AND INSIGHTS:

1. LUBRICATION FAILURES are the leading cause of bearing and mechanical failures (35% of all failures). Key prevention: automated lubrication systems, grease line monitoring, oil analysis programs.

2. WATER/COOLING SYSTEM ISSUES account for 25% of failures. Scale buildup, water quality, and leak detection are critical maintenance areas.

3. SEAL AND GASKET WEAR follows predictable patterns — implementing time-based replacement before end-of-life prevents 90% of seal-related failures.

4. PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE (vibration, oil analysis, thermography) has proven effective in preventing 80% of potential catastrophic failures when properly implemented.

5. MONITORING GAPS are a common contributing factor — installing redundant sensors and automated trending alarms significantly reduces risk.
