STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: HYDRAULIC SYSTEM MAINTENANCE
============================================================
Document ID: SOP-HYD-002
Revision: 2.0
Effective Date: March 2026
Department: Hydraulic & Pneumatic Maintenance

1. PURPOSE
----------
This SOP establishes procedures for routine maintenance, troubleshooting, and repair of hydraulic systems used in rolling mills, presses, and continuous casting machines.

2. SCOPE
--------
Applies to all hydraulic systems including:
- Rolling mill AGC (Automatic Gauge Control) hydraulics (HRM-001, CRM-001)
- Hydraulic press main cylinders (HP-001)
- Caster mould oscillation hydraulics (CCM-001)
- Ladle furnace electrode positioning (LF-001)

3. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------
- Hydraulic systems operate at pressures up to 315 bar — LETHAL INJECTION HAZARD
- NEVER use hands to check for leaks — use cardboard or paper
- Depressurize system completely before opening any connection
- Use rated safety blocks when working under raised hydraulic components
- Wear safety glasses and face shield when working with pressurized fittings

4. ROUTINE MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
---------------------------------

Daily checks:
- Oil level in reservoir (maintain between min/max marks)
- System pressure gauge reading (compare with setpoint)
- Visual inspection for leaks at fittings, hoses, cylinders
- Check oil temperature (normal: 40-55°C)
- Listen for pump cavitation noise

Weekly checks:
- Check filter differential pressure indicators
- Inspect accumulator pre-charge pressure (nitrogen)
- Verify all safety valve settings
- Check hose condition for bulging, cracking, or abrasion

Monthly checks:
- Oil sample for laboratory analysis
- Filter element replacement (if differential pressure indicator triggered)
- Cylinder rod inspection (scoring, pitting, chrome flaking)
- Check all bolted connections for tightness
- Test emergency stop and safety interlocks

Quarterly checks:
- Servo valve testing (frequency response, null bias)
- Pump performance test (flow rate vs pressure)
- Accumulator bladder/diaphragm inspection
- Complete hose inventory and condition assessment

5. OIL ANALYSIS PARAMETERS
----------------------------
Parameter                    | Action Level
-----------------------------|-------------------
Particle count (ISO 4406)    | > 18/16/13 — change filters
Water content                | > 0.1% — investigate source
Viscosity change             | > ±10% — investigate
Acid number increase         | > 0.5 mg KOH/g — consider oil change
Iron particles               | > 50 ppm — check pump/valve wear
Copper particles             | > 20 ppm — check bearings/bushings

6. TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
--------------------------

Problem: System pressure low
→ Check pump output (flow test)
→ Inspect relief valve setting
→ Check for internal leaks in cylinders (bypass test)
→ Verify oil level and suction strainer

Problem: Oil temperature high (>65°C)
→ Check oil cooler (water flow, fouling)
→ Verify reservoir oil level
→ Check for internal leaks causing heat generation
→ Reduce system duty cycle if possible
→ Inspect pump for wear (increased leakage generates heat)

Problem: Jerky cylinder movement
→ Check for air in system (bleed at highest point)
→ Inspect servo/proportional valve for contamination
→ Check cylinder seals for damage
→ Verify hydraulic fluid viscosity

Problem: Pump noise (cavitation)
→ Check suction line for air leaks
→ Verify oil viscosity (cold oil too thick)
→ Clean or replace suction strainer
→ Check oil level (suction line must be submerged)

Problem: Cylinder drift (cannot hold position)
→ Check piston seals
→ Inspect pilot-operated check valves
→ Test counterbalance valves
→ Check for servo valve leakage past null

7. HYDRAULIC CYLINDER SEAL REPLACEMENT
-----------------------------------------
Step 1: Depressurize system completely
Step 2: Disconnect hydraulic lines (cap open ports to prevent contamination)
Step 3: Remove cylinder from machine (note orientation and position)
Step 4: Disassemble cylinder (remove gland, extract piston)
Step 5: Inspect bore for scoring (light honing if needed)
Step 6: Inspect piston rod for damage (chrome repair if needed)
Step 7: Install new seal kit (correct orientation for each seal)
Step 8: Reassemble with clean oil on all seals
Step 9: Reinstall and reconnect lines
Step 10: Bleed air from cylinder and test at low pressure first

8. HYDRAULIC OIL SPECIFICATION
--------------------------------
- Type: ISO VG 46 anti-wear hydraulic oil (HLP 46)
- Operating temperature range: 15-65°C
- Cleanliness target: ISO 16/14/11
- Water content: <0.05% (target)
- Reservoir capacity: varies by system (check system drawing)
- Oil change interval: 4000 hours or annually, whichever comes first

9. ESTIMATED DURATION AND RESOURCES
--------------------------------------
- Routine daily/weekly checks: 1-2 hours per system
- Monthly inspection: 4 hours per system
- Filter change: 1 hour
- Oil change (complete): 4-8 hours depending on reservoir size
- Cylinder seal replacement: 6-12 hours
- Servo valve replacement: 2-4 hours
