STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: COOLING SYSTEM MAINTENANCE
==========================================================
Document ID: SOP-UTIL-003
Revision: 2.1
Effective Date: February 2026
Department: Utilities Maintenance

1. PURPOSE
----------
This SOP covers maintenance procedures for industrial cooling systems including cooling towers, heat exchangers, and closed-loop cooling circuits serving the steel plant.

2. SCOPE
--------
Applies to:
- Cooling Tower #3 (CT-001) — Casting and Rolling area
- Mould cooling circuits (CCM-001)
- Rolling mill roll cooling systems (HRM-001, CRM-001)
- Furnace cooling water circuits (BF-001, BOF-001, LF-001)

3. COOLING TOWER MAINTENANCE
------------------------------

3.1 Daily Checks
- Basin water level
- Make-up water flow rate
- Fan operation (noise, vibration)
- Blow-down rate and conductivity
- Approach temperature (outlet - ambient wet bulb)
- Visual drift eliminator condition

3.2 Weekly Checks
- Chemical treatment levels (pH, conductivity, biocide residual)
- Fill media condition (visual from access door)
- Distribution nozzle spray pattern
- Basin sediment level
- Fan belt tension (if belt-driven)

3.3 Monthly Checks
- Fan motor bearing temperature and vibration
- Gearbox oil level and condition
- Fill media sections for fouling or algae growth
- Structure integrity (corrosion, fastener condition)
- Drift eliminator condition

3.4 Quarterly Checks
- Full water chemistry analysis
- Fan blade inspection (pitch, balance, cracks)
- Motor current and power factor measurement
- Heat rejection capacity test

3.5 Annual Checks
- Complete basin drain, clean, and inspect
- Fill media detailed inspection (consider replacement if >15% degradation)
- Fan motor overhaul
- Gearbox oil change
- Structural inspection (concrete, steel, FRP)
- Legionella risk assessment

4. WATER TREATMENT
-------------------
Parameter                    | Target Range
-----------------------------|-------------------
pH                           | 7.0 - 8.5
Conductivity                 | < 2500 µS/cm
Total hardness               | < 500 ppm as CaCO3
Chloride                     | < 250 ppm
Free chlorine (biocide)      | 0.5 - 1.0 ppm
Cycles of concentration      | 3 - 5
Langelier Saturation Index   | -0.5 to +0.5

Chemical treatment program:
- Scale inhibitor: Phosphonate-based, continuous dosing
- Corrosion inhibitor: Molybdate + phosphate blend
- Biocide: Alternating oxidizing (chlorine) and non-oxidizing (isothiazolone)
- Dispersant: Polymer-based, prevents fouling

5. CLOSED-LOOP COOLING CIRCUITS
----------------------------------

Mould cooling (CCM-001):
- Water quality: Demineralized, conductivity < 20 µS/cm
- Flow rate: As per strand requirement (critical for shell formation)
- Temperature rise: Max 8°C across mould
- Pressure: 6-10 bar at mould inlet
- CRITICAL: Any flow reduction triggers automatic casting speed reduction

Roll cooling (HRM-001):
- Water quality: Filtered to 50 micron minimum
- Flow rate: 200-400 L/min per stand
- Temperature: 25-35°C supply
- Additives: Corrosion inhibitor as required
- Check nozzle spray pattern weekly

6. TROUBLESHOOTING
-------------------

Problem: Cooling tower approach temperature increasing
→ Check fill media for fouling or degradation
→ Verify fan speed and blade pitch
→ Check water distribution (nozzle blockage)
→ Review blow-down rate and cycles of concentration
→ Check for air recirculation (wind direction effects)

Problem: High conductivity in cooling water
→ Increase blow-down rate
→ Check make-up water quality
→ Review chemical dosing rates
→ Check for process water contamination

Problem: Mould cooling flow low
→ Check pump performance
→ Inspect strainer and filters
→ Check for scale buildup in mould channels
→ Verify control valve position
→ URGENT: Reduce casting speed if flow cannot be restored

Problem: Fan motor high vibration
→ Check fan blade balance and pitch
→ Inspect motor bearings
→ Check gearbox alignment
→ Verify mounting bolt tightness
→ Check for ice formation in cold weather

7. FILL MEDIA REPLACEMENT CRITERIA
-------------------------------------
Replace fill media when:
- More than 15% of fill area shows collapse or deformation
- Approach temperature increases by more than 3°C from design
- Biological fouling cannot be controlled by chemical treatment
- Physical damage from ice or debris
- Age exceeds manufacturer's recommended life (typically 10-15 years)

8. ESTIMATED DURATION AND RESOURCES
--------------------------------------
- Daily/weekly checks: 1-2 hours
- Monthly inspection: 4 hours
- Annual basin clean: 2-3 days
- Fill media replacement: 5-7 days
- Fan motor replacement: 8-12 hours
- Personnel: 2 maintenance technicians for routine, 4-6 for major work
