STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE: BEARING REPLACEMENT
===================================================
Document ID: SOP-MECH-001
Revision: 3.0
Effective Date: January 2026
Department: Mechanical Maintenance

1. PURPOSE
----------
This SOP defines the step-by-step procedure for replacing rolling element bearings on continuous caster segment rollers, rolling mill backup rolls, and other critical rotating equipment.

2. SCOPE
--------
Applies to all SKF, FAG, and NSK spherical roller bearings (series 22000, 23000, 24000) installed on:
- Continuous caster segment rollers (CCM-001)
- Rolling mill backup roll assemblies (HRM-001, CRM-001)
- Crane hoist mechanisms (CR-001)

3. SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------
- Lock Out / Tag Out (LOTO) must be applied before any work
- Minimum 2 personnel required for bearing replacement
- Personal Protective Equipment: Safety helmet, steel-toe boots, heat-resistant gloves, safety glasses
- Ensure equipment is fully de-energized and cooled below 50°C
- Obtain work permit from shift supervisor
- Verify crane availability for heavy component handling

4. TOOLS AND MATERIALS REQUIRED
--------------------------------
- Hydraulic bearing puller set (up to 500mm bore)
- Induction bearing heater (up to 200°C)
- Torque wrench (range: 200-1500 Nm)
- Dial indicator with magnetic base
- Feeler gauge set (0.02-1.00 mm)
- Bearing grease: SKF LGMT 3 or equivalent
- Cleaning solvent (non-chlorinated)
- Lint-free wipes
- New bearings (verified part number and dimensions)
- New seals and O-rings
- Lifting slings and shackles (rated for component weight)

5. PRE-REPLACEMENT CHECKS
---------------------------
Step 1: Verify bearing failure diagnosis
  - Review vibration analysis report
  - Check temperature trend data
  - Confirm bearing identification (part number, position)
  
Step 2: Prepare replacement bearing
  - Inspect new bearing for shipping damage
  - Verify part number matches specification
  - Check bearing clearance (C3 or C4 as specified)
  - Store in clean, dry location until installation

Step 3: Plan the work
  - Estimate downtime (typical: 4-6 hours per bearing)
  - Arrange crane and rigging
  - Notify production planning of outage window
  - Prepare tools and workspace

6. REMOVAL PROCEDURE
---------------------
Step 1: Isolate and secure equipment
  - Apply LOTO on all energy sources
  - Drain lubricant/coolant from bearing housing
  - Allow cooling to below 50°C

Step 2: Remove housing and covers
  - Unbolt bearing housing cap bolts in star pattern
  - Lift housing cover using crane if heavy
  - Clean exposed bearing area

Step 3: Remove old bearing
  - Use hydraulic bearing puller
  - Apply even force to avoid shaft damage
  - NEVER use hammers or impact tools on precision surfaces
  - Inspect shaft surface after bearing removal
  - Document any shaft damage (pitting, scoring, wear)

Step 4: Inspect shaft and housing
  - Measure shaft diameter at bearing seat (3 positions)
  - Measure housing bore diameter (3 positions)
  - Compare with specification tolerances
  - Check for fretting corrosion, scoring, or wear
  - If out of tolerance: Report to engineering for disposition

7. INSTALLATION PROCEDURE
---------------------------
Step 1: Clean all surfaces
  - Clean shaft bearing seat with solvent
  - Clean housing bore
  - Remove all old grease and debris
  - Dry completely with lint-free wipes

Step 2: Heat new bearing
  - Use induction heater to heat bearing to 80-100°C above ambient
  - NEVER exceed 125°C (risk of bearing damage)
  - Handle with heat-resistant gloves
  - Work quickly — bearing contracts as it cools

Step 3: Mount bearing on shaft
  - Slide heated bearing onto shaft smoothly
  - Push firmly against shaft shoulder
  - Hold in position until bearing cools and grips shaft
  - Verify bearing is fully seated against shoulder

Step 4: Install seals
  - Install new seals with correct orientation (lip facing lubricant side)
  - Ensure seals are not damaged during installation
  - Apply thin layer of grease to seal lip

Step 5: Reassemble housing
  - Lower shaft assembly into housing
  - Install housing cap
  - Tighten bolts in star pattern to specified torque
  - Verify shaft rotates freely by hand

Step 6: Lubricate
  - Fill bearing housing with specified grease quantity (typically 30-50% of free space)
  - DO NOT over-grease (causes overheating)
  - Connect grease lines if applicable
  - Verify grease reaches bearing through inspection port

8. POST-INSTALLATION VERIFICATION
------------------------------------
Step 1: Alignment check
  - Use dial indicator to check shaft runout (<0.05 mm)
  - Verify axial float is within specification
  - Check coupling alignment if applicable

Step 2: Trial run
  - Remove LOTO
  - Start equipment at LOW speed for first 30 minutes
  - Monitor bearing temperature (should not exceed ambient + 40°C)
  - Listen for abnormal noise
  - Check vibration levels (should be below 2.0 mm/s)

Step 3: Documentation
  - Record bearing part number, manufacturer, batch
  - Record installation date and personnel
  - Record initial vibration and temperature readings
  - Update equipment maintenance log
  - Update bearing life tracking database

9. FAILURE ANALYSIS OF REMOVED BEARING
-----------------------------------------
Always perform failure analysis on removed bearings:
- Photograph the bearing before cleaning
- Identify failure mode: fatigue spalling, contamination, lubrication failure, overload, corrosion
- Record findings in failure analysis report
- Use findings to prevent recurrence (root cause correction)

10. ESTIMATED DURATION AND RESOURCES
--------------------------------------
- Standard bearing replacement: 4-6 hours
- Complex replacement (caster segment): 6-10 hours
- Personnel: 2 mechanics + 1 crane operator
- Typical cost: Parts + 8 man-hours labor
