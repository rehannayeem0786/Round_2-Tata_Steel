BLAST FURNACE #1 — EQUIPMENT MANUAL
=====================================
Model: BF-5000 Series
Manufacturer: Danieli Corus
Installation Date: March 2015
Location: Ironmaking Zone, Steel Plant

1. EQUIPMENT OVERVIEW
---------------------
The Blast Furnace #1 is the primary ironmaking unit with a working volume of 2500 m³ and a daily capacity of 5000 tonnes of hot metal. The furnace operates continuously, converting iron ore, coke, and fluxes into liquid pig iron (hot metal) and slag.

2. OPERATING PARAMETERS
-----------------------
- Hot blast temperature: 1150-1250°C (normal operating range)
- Top gas pressure: 2.0-2.8 bar
- Hearth temperature: 1450-1520°C
- Tuyere wind velocity: 200-240 m/s
- Coke rate: 350-400 kg/tonne hot metal
- Slag basicity (CaO/SiO2): 1.0-1.2
- Hot metal silicon: 0.3-0.6%
- Hot metal temperature: 1480-1520°C

3. CRITICAL COMPONENTS AND FAILURE MODES
-----------------------------------------

3.1 Tuyere Assembly
- Function: Injects preheated air (hot blast) into the furnace
- Common failures: Burn-through due to cooling water failure, erosion from burden contact
- Warning signs: Increased cooling water temperature, water flow decrease, visual burn marks
- Recommended inspection: Every 2 weeks visual, every 3 months detailed
- Typical life: 12-18 months depending on operating conditions

3.2 Cooling System (Stave Coolers)
- Function: Maintains furnace shell temperature within safe limits
- Common failures: Water leak from cracked staves, blocked cooling channels
- Warning signs: Hot spots on shell (detected by thermocouples), water quality deterioration
- Recommended inspection: Continuous monitoring via embedded thermocouples
- Critical action: If shell temperature exceeds 350°C, reduce production rate

3.3 Burden Distribution System (Bell-less Top / Paul Wurth)
- Function: Controls the distribution of raw materials in the furnace
- Common failures: Chute actuator failure, distribution plate wear, gear mechanism jamming
- Warning signs: Uneven gas distribution (detected by top gas temperature probes), abnormal chute position signals
- Recommended inspection: Monthly mechanical inspection, daily position calibration check

3.4 Tap Hole Assembly
- Function: Opening for draining hot metal and slag
- Common failures: Tap hole drill failure, mud gun malfunction, taphole clay issues
- Warning signs: Difficulty in opening/closing, irregular tapping time, hot metal breakthrough risk
- Recommended inspection: After every cast (4-6 times per day)

3.5 Hot Blast Stoves
- Function: Preheats combustion air to 1200°C before injection
- Common failures: Refractory failure, dome cracking, checker brick collapse
- Warning signs: Decreased blast temperature, increased fuel consumption, temperature imbalance between stoves
- Recommended inspection: Annual internal inspection during planned shutdown

4. VIBRATION ANALYSIS GUIDELINES
---------------------------------
- Normal operating vibration: 0.5-3.0 mm/s RMS
- Warning threshold: 5.0 mm/s RMS
- Critical threshold: 7.0 mm/s RMS
- Alarm threshold: 10.0 mm/s RMS (automatic trip)

Vibration sources to investigate:
- Uneven burden distribution causing gas channeling
- Cooling system pump issues
- Hot blast valve malfunction
- Structural resonance in the furnace body

5. MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
-----------------------
Daily: Visual inspection of tuyeres, tap holes, cooling water system
Weekly: Burden distribution system calibration, gas analysis
Monthly: Detailed mechanical inspection of top equipment, cooling system assessment
Quarterly: Hot blast stove inspection, refractory survey
Annual: Complete furnace survey during planned shutdown (7-14 days)

6. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
------------------------
6.1 Cooling Water Failure
- IMMEDIATE: Reduce blast volume by 50%
- Within 5 min: Activate emergency cooling loop
- Within 15 min: If not restored, begin controlled banking of furnace
- DO NOT: Shut off blast completely without banking procedure

6.2 Tuyere Burn-Through
- IMMEDIATE: Isolate affected tuyere cooling circuit
- Plug the tuyere from outside
- Continue operation on remaining tuyeres if possible
- Schedule replacement during next planned maintenance window

6.3 Tap Hole Breakthrough
- IMMEDIATE: Sound emergency alarm, evacuate cast house floor
- Activate emergency slag runner water spray
- Attempt to close with mud gun
- Contact emergency response team

7. SPARE PARTS — CRITICAL INVENTORY
------------------------------------
- Tuyere noses: Minimum 6 in stock
- Stave cooler segments: Minimum 2 per zone
- Tap hole drill bits: Minimum 4
- Mud gun nozzles: Minimum 3
- Burden distribution chute actuator motor: 1 spare
- Hot blast valve: 1 complete spare assembly
- Cooling water pump: 1 standby pump per circuit
