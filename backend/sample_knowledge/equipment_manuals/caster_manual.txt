CONTINUOUS CASTING MACHINE #1 — EQUIPMENT MANUAL
=================================================
Model: 6-Strand Billet Caster with EM Stirring
Manufacturer: Primetals Technologies
Installation Date: January 2017
Location: Casting Zone, Steel Plant

1. EQUIPMENT OVERVIEW
---------------------
The Continuous Casting Machine (CCM-001) is a 6-strand billet caster producing 150x150 mm billets at a maximum casting speed of 2.5 m/min per strand. Equipped with electromagnetic stirring (EMS) for improved internal quality. Annual capacity: 1.5 million tonnes.

2. OPERATING PARAMETERS
-----------------------
- Tundish temperature: 1530-1560°C
- Casting speed: 1.8-2.5 m/min (grade dependent)
- Mould oscillation: 120-200 cycles/min
- Mould level control: ±2 mm from setpoint
- Secondary cooling: Air-mist system, 6 zones per strand
- Billet straightening: 5-point progressive straightening
- Specific water flow: 0.5-0.8 L/kg steel

3. CRITICAL COMPONENTS AND FAILURE MODES
-----------------------------------------

3.1 Copper Mould Assembly
- Function: Primary solidification of steel, forms initial shell
- Common failures: Copper plate cracking, wear groove formation, coating failure
- Warning signs: Mould level instability, increased thermocouple temperature variation, surface quality deterioration
- Copper plate life: 40,000-60,000 tonnes (narrow face), 80,000-100,000 tonnes (wide face)
- Recommended: Track mould thermal map, inspect during every campaign change

3.2 Mould Oscillation System
- Function: Prevents sticking of solidifying shell to mould
- Common failures: Hydraulic cylinder failure, spring breakage, guide bearing wear
- Warning signs: Oscillation frequency deviation, negative strip time variation, sticker alarm frequency increase
- Recommended: Weekly oscillation parameter check, monthly mechanism inspection

3.3 Segment Roller Bearings
- Function: Support and guide the billet through the machine
- Common failures: Bearing seizure, race spalling, seal failure leading to water ingress
- Warning signs: Vibration increase, temperature rise, abnormal noise, bearing rotation stiffness
- Bearing life: 12-18 months typical (varies with cooling water quality)
- Critical: Water ingress is the #1 cause of premature bearing failure

3.4 Submerged Entry Nozzle (SEN) / Tundish Nozzle
- Function: Controls steel flow from tundish to mould
- Common failures: Clogging (alumina buildup), erosion, cracking
- Warning signs: Mould level hunting, inconsistent flow, strand speed deviation
- Nozzle life: 6-10 heats depending on steel grade
- Recommended: Argon injection rate optimization to prevent clogging

3.5 Secondary Cooling System
- Function: Controlled solidification rate below the mould
- Common failures: Nozzle blockage, header valve failure, zone temperature deviation
- Warning signs: Surface cracking on billets, bulging, internal quality defects
- Recommended: Weekly nozzle spray pattern inspection, monthly flow calibration

3.6 Electromagnetic Stirrer (EMS)
- Function: Improves internal quality by stirring liquid steel
- Common failures: Coil insulation breakdown, power supply failure, cooling water leak
- Warning signs: Deteriorated internal quality (macro etch), power reading deviation
- Recommended: Monthly insulation resistance measurement, quarterly coil inspection

4. VIBRATION ANALYSIS — SEGMENT ROLLERS
-----------------------------------------
- Normal: 0.2-1.5 mm/s RMS
- Warning: 3.5 mm/s RMS
- Critical: 4.5 mm/s RMS (schedule bearing replacement)
- Alarm: 6.0 mm/s RMS (stop strand for immediate replacement)

Bearing failure progression:
Stage 1 (Weeks before failure): Slight increase in ultrasonic readings
Stage 2 (Days before failure): Vibration increase visible in velocity spectrum
Stage 3 (Hours before failure): Temperature rise, audible noise
Stage 4 (Imminent): Rapid vibration spike, seizure risk

5. MOULD LEVEL CONTROL
-----------------------
Stable mould level is critical for quality. Deviations indicate:
- ±1mm: Normal operating variation
- ±2mm: Investigate SEN condition, flow control
- ±3mm: WARNING — potential sticking risk, check immediately
- ±5mm: CRITICAL — risk of breakout, reduce casting speed or stop strand

6. MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
-----------------------
Every heat: SEN/nozzle change (if required), tundish inspection
Daily: Mould oscillation check, secondary cooling visual inspection
Weekly: Segment roller rotation check, spray nozzle testing
Monthly: Bearing vibration survey, mould thermocouple calibration
Quarterly: Mould copper plate measurement, EMS inspection
Semi-annual: Segment alignment survey, roller replacement campaign
Annual: Complete machine overhaul (per strand, rotating schedule)

7. COMMON FAILURE SCENARIOS AND ROOT CAUSES
---------------------------------------------

Scenario: Strand Breakout
Root causes: 
- Mould level control failure
- SEN clogging causing uneven flow
- Mould lubrication failure
- Copper plate crack allowing water contact
Prevention: Maintain sticker detection system, regular mould inspection

Scenario: Bearing Seizure on Segment Roller
Root causes:
- Water ingress through damaged seals
- Inadequate lubrication (blocked grease line)  
- Excessive thermal load from improper secondary cooling
- Extended operation beyond bearing life
Prevention: Regular vibration monitoring, seal inspection, grease system maintenance

Scenario: Internal Cracks in Billet
Root causes:
- Excessive casting speed for the grade
- Improper secondary cooling intensity
- EMS malfunction causing insufficient stirring
- High superheat temperature
Prevention: Optimize cooling curves per grade, maintain EMS, control tundish temperature
