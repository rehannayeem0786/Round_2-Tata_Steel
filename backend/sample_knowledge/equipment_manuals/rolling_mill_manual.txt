HOT ROLLING MILL — EQUIPMENT MANUAL
====================================
Model: Continuous Hot Strip Mill — 6-Stand Finishing
Manufacturer: SMS Group
Installation Date: May 2016
Location: Rolling Zone, Steel Plant

1. EQUIPMENT OVERVIEW
---------------------
The Hot Rolling Mill (HRM) is a continuous hot strip mill consisting of a roughing section, crop shear, 6-stand finishing mill, laminar cooling system, and downcoilers. It processes reheated slabs into hot-rolled coils with thickness ranging from 1.2 mm to 16 mm.

2. OPERATING PARAMETERS
-----------------------
- Slab entry temperature: 1050-1100°C
- Finishing temperature: 830-900°C
- Coiling temperature: 550-700°C (grade dependent)
- Maximum strip width: 1850 mm
- Maximum rolling speed: 20 m/s
- Annual capacity: 4 million tonnes

3. CRITICAL COMPONENTS AND FAILURE MODES
-----------------------------------------

3.1 Work Rolls (Finishing Stands F1-F6)
- Function: Direct contact with strip for thickness reduction
- Common failures: Surface spalling, thermal cracking, bearing failure
- Warning signs: Strip surface defects, increased roll force, vibration increase, bearing temperature rise
- Roll change frequency: Every 100-150 km of rolled strip
- Recommended: Monitor roll force trends, surface inspection after each campaign

3.2 Backup Rolls
- Function: Support work rolls and maintain flatness
- Common failures: Spalling, peeling, bearing damage
- Warning signs: Strip flatness deterioration, unusual vibration patterns
- Campaign length: 15,000-20,000 tonnes of strip
- Critical: Inspect bearing seats during every change

3.3 Hydraulic AGC (Automatic Gauge Control)
- Function: Controls strip thickness with hydraulic cylinders
- Common failures: Servo valve malfunction, cylinder seal leak, position sensor drift
- Warning signs: Thickness variation beyond tolerance (±0.02 mm), hydraulic pressure fluctuation
- Recommended inspection: Monthly calibration, quarterly servo valve testing

3.4 Motor and Drive System
- Function: Provides rolling force via electric motors and gear spindles
- Common failures: Motor winding insulation failure, gear tooth wear, spindle coupling failure
- Warning signs: Motor current spikes (>500A sustained), abnormal noise, gear oil analysis showing metal particles
- Recommended: Online motor current monitoring, quarterly oil analysis, annual thermography

3.5 Laminar Cooling System
- Function: Controlled cooling of strip between finishing mill and coiler
- Common failures: Nozzle blockage, header valve failure, flow distribution imbalance
- Warning signs: Inconsistent strip temperature, coiling temperature deviation, strip flatness issues
- Recommended: Weekly nozzle flow check, monthly header inspection

4. VIBRATION ANALYSIS GUIDELINES
---------------------------------
- Normal: 0.5-3.5 mm/s RMS on bearing housing
- Pre-warning: 4.5 mm/s
- Warning: 6.0 mm/s
- Critical: 8.5 mm/s (schedule immediate inspection)
- Alarm: 12.0 mm/s (automatic mill stop)

Key vibration frequencies to monitor:
- 1x RPM: Imbalance or misalignment
- 2x RPM: Looseness or misalignment
- BPFO/BPFI: Bearing defect frequencies
- Gear mesh frequency: Gear tooth damage

5. LUBRICATION REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------
- Work roll bearings: Grease lubrication, automatic system
- Backup roll bearings: Oil film bearing, circulation system
- Gearboxes: EP gear oil, ISO VG 320
- Oil analysis interval: Monthly
- Grease replenishment: Daily (automated)

6. MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
-----------------------
Every roll change: Work roll bearing inspection, strip guide alignment
Weekly: Hydraulic system pressure check, cooling nozzle flow test
Monthly: AGC calibration, motor temperature trend review, oil analysis
Quarterly: Servo valve testing, gear spindle inspection
Semi-annual: Drive motor inspection, gear reducer inspection
Annual: Complete mill alignment survey, backup roll bearing replacement

7. TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
--------------------------

Problem: Strip thickness variation
→ Check AGC hydraulic pressure
→ Verify position sensor calibration
→ Inspect servo valves for sticking
→ Check work roll profile (thermal crown)

Problem: Excessive vibration on finishing stand
→ Check roll balance and roundness
→ Inspect bearings (vibration signature analysis)
→ Verify spindle coupling alignment
→ Check for strip cobble damage

Problem: Motor current exceeding limits
→ Check for excessive reduction schedule
→ Verify lubricant flow to roll bite
→ Inspect motor cooling system
→ Check drive alignment

Problem: Strip surface defects (scale marks)
→ Check descaler spray pressure and pattern
→ Verify water quality (no nozzle blockage)
→ Inspect roll surface condition
→ Check slab heating uniformity

8. SPARE PARTS — CRITICAL INVENTORY
------------------------------------
- Work rolls: 2 sets per stand
- Backup rolls: 1 spare set per stand
- AGC servo valves: 2 per stand
- Hydraulic cylinder seals: Complete kit per stand
- Motor bearings: 1 set per motor
- Spindle couplings: 1 spare per stand
- Cooling nozzles: 10% spare inventory
