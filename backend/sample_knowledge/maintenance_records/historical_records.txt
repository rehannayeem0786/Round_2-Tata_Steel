RECORD #MR-2024-001
Equipment: Blast Furnace #1 (BF-001)
Date: 2024-03-15
Type: Corrective Maintenance
Issue: Tuyere #7 burn-through
Description: Tuyere #7 experienced complete burn-through due to cooling water blockage. Hot metal splash observed from tuyere area. Emergency plug installed.
Root Cause: Calcium carbonate scale buildup in tuyere cooling channel, blocking water flow. Water treatment program was inadequate.
Actions Taken: Replaced tuyere nose assembly, cleared cooling channel, replaced water supply hose. Installed water quality monitoring sensor on tuyere cooling circuit.
Downtime: 6 hours (tuyere replacement during scheduled inter-cast period)
Parts Used: 1x tuyere nose (copper), 1x water hose assembly, gasket set
Lesson Learned: Implement weekly water quality check specifically for tuyere cooling circuits. Added to preventive maintenance checklist.

RECORD #MR-2024-002
Equipment: Continuous Caster #1 (CCM-001)
Date: 2024-06-22
Type: Corrective Maintenance (Emergency)
Issue: Strand 2 breakout
Description: Liquid steel breakout occurred at strand 2, 1.5m below mould. Caused by shell sticking in mould due to lubrication failure. Casting stopped on all strands as safety precaution.
Root Cause: Mould oscillation oil pump failure caused loss of lubrication. Sticker detection system alarmed 3 seconds before breakout but automatic speed reduction was too slow.
Actions Taken: Cleaned breakout area (12 hours). Replaced oscillation oil pump. Installed redundant backup pump. Updated sticker detection response time from 3s to 1s automatic speed reduction. Replaced damaged mould copper plates.
Downtime: 36 hours total (cleaning + repair + restart)
Parts Used: 1x oil pump, 2x mould copper plates, strand guide rollers (6x damaged)
Lesson Learned: Installed redundant oscillation pump with automatic switchover. Reduced sticker detection response time. Added oscillation pump pressure to critical alarm list.
Cost Impact: Estimated loss of 2,500 tonnes production + repair costs ~$150,000

RECORD #MR-2025-001

Date: 2025-02-18
Type: Corrective Maintenance
Issue: Main cylinder drift / cannot hold position
Description: Press unable to maintain position under load. Gradual drift of approximately 2mm/minute observed. Oil temperature elevated (62°C).
Root Cause: Piston seal failure on main cylinder. Age-related degradation of polyurethane seals. Internal leakage generating excess heat.

Downtime: 14 hours

RECORD #MR-2026-001
Equipment: Blast Furnace #1 (BF-001)
Date: 2026-01-10
Type: Corrective Maintenance (Emergency)
Issue: Burden distribution chute actuator failure
Description: Chute actuator motor seized, causing the distribution chute to lock in one position. This resulted in uneven burden distribution and gas channeling in the furnace.
Symptoms observed: Uneven top gas temperature (variation >50°C between probes), increased pressure fluctuations, abnormal gas flow pattern.
Root Cause: Actuator motor bearing failure due to high-temperature exposure. Motor cooling fan had partially blocked air intake.
Actions Taken: Replaced actuator motor and position sensor. Cleaned motor cooling fan inlet. Installed temperature sensor on motor housing. Calibrated chute angles. Added motor temperature to alarm system.
Downtime: 8 hours (reduced blast volume during repair)
Parts Used: Chute actuator motor, position sensor, cooling fan filter
Lesson Learned: Add motor temperature monitoring for all furnace top equipment. Include motor cooling system in routine inspection.
